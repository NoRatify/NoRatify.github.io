What?! But this is a historic, monumental achievement!

Indeed, academic workers have been under UC's foot for so long that even small concessions seem like a win.

What has the bargaining team accomplished?

  • Increased paid parental leave (from 4 weeks to 8 weeks) for all Postdocs
  • Marginally improved security for international postdocs
  • Some improvements around grievance arbitration and anti-bullying/abusive conduct measures

How does the current agreement fail us?

Wages

This is the big one. We’ve been told that “most Postdocs will receive a 20-23% salary increase” by October 2023. This is misleading. Here’s why:

  • The “20-23% salary increase” estimate combines step increases, based on experience, with overall scale increases, meant to offset inflation and cost of living
  • The scale increases–which represent the true “growth” in our pay scale–will be instituted in two phases: ~8% in April and ~8% in October, for a total of ~16% by October 2023

When we look at the scale increases, accounting for inflation, the current proposed pay scale constitutes a pay cut for Postdocs:

  • Inflation is bad now, and has been for some time. Inflation over the past year, while this contract was negotiated, was ~7.7%. Inflation the previous year was ~5.4%. The Social Security Administration issued a 5.9% COLA increase for 2021 and an 8.7% COLA increase for 2022
  • If inflation in 2023 remains steady, the proposed initial 16% increase from our current pay scale over the year-long phase-in period constitutes at best, wage stagnation, and at worst, a pay cut
  • Following this initial phase-in period, scale increases will be 3.7% per year, far below the current rate of inflation

This pay scale not only doesn’t make up for the effective wage decreases we’ve experienced over the past several years due to our previous contract not keeping up with inflation, it potentially makes the problem worse with scale increases below the current rate of inflation.

The result is that the proposed scale constitutes a pay cut for all Postdocs that gets progressively worse over the course of the contract. 

Childcare

The TA includes a childcare subsidy that starts at $2,500 per YEAR. In many cities where there are UC campuses, childcare for a single child can cost upwards of $2,000 per MONTH. This subsidy does little to reduce the burden on Postdoc parents. Moreover, this benefit is a subsidy for licensed childcare providers, meaning that any Postdoc parent who cannot afford childcare from a licensed provider (which is often the UC system itself) will receive no additional monetary compensation above their salary to help pay for costs associated with raising a child.
